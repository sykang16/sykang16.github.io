About

For this project, we have utilized the Crime Data from 2020 to Present, which provides detailed records of reported criminal incidents across various geographic areas within Los Angeles. The dataset captures essential information about crime occurrences, including dates, times, locations, types of crimes, and demographic information related to victims.

This comprehensive dataset serves as a valuable resource for analyzing crime patterns, identifying hotspots, and exploring correlations between crime rates and temporal or geographic factors.

    • Crime Trend Over Years

      Insights
  • CrimeActivity Patterns: Theincrease in crime counts from 2020 to 2022 may reflect heightened criminal activity or improved reporting mechanisms during this period.
  • Reasonsfor Decline: Thedecline post-2022 could be attributed to stricter law enforcement, community-based crime prevention efforts, or possible inconsistencies in reporting or data collection.
  • Incomplete Data in 2024: Thesteep drop in crime counts in 2024 may indicate incomplete data for the year, as the dataset appears to end mid-year.
  • Pandemic Influence: Theincrease from 2020 to 2021 suggests a gradual recovery of crime rates after the initial impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic, with restrictions easing and normal activities resuming by 2022

      Insight

    • Crime Trends Over Years by AREA

      Insights
  • Targeted Crime Reduction: High-crime areas like 77th Street and Central require focused intervention strategies, such as tailored law enforcement or community programs.

  • Safer Areas: Consistently low crime counts in areas like Topanga and West LA could provide insights into effective crime prevention measures that can be replicated in high-crime areas.

    Crime Forcasting with ARIMA

    The ARIMA model was used to forecast daily crime counts for the next 30 days, from late October 2024 to late November 2024.

      Key Observations
  • Forecasted values indicate a continued downward trend in daily crime counts.
  • Confidence intervals were included to indicate the reliability of the predictions.
  • The graph : Forecast of Crime Counts with Confidence Intervals.
      • Conclusions
  • The ARIMA(1,1,1) model captured the crime trends effectively and provided reliable short-term forecasts.
  • Crime counts are predicted to remain low in the near future, which aligns with the recent observed trend in 2024.
  • Confidence intervals highlight the potential uncertainty in predictions, emphasizing the need for continuous monitoring.
